Overview

What is a Medical Weight Loss Program?

Medical weight loss is a provider-guided program designed to support healthy, sustainable weight management through individualized treatment strategies. At INFUDERM, we combine medical expertise, lifestyle guidance, and GLP-1 medications to create customized plans based on your unique goals, health history, and needs.

What are GLP-1 Injections? GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) medications are prescription treatments that help regulate appetite and support weight management. These medications work by mimicking naturally occurring hormones involved in hunger and digestion, helping patients feel fuller for longer and better manage food intake.

Treatment options may include Semaglutide and Tirzepatide, depending on your provider's evaluation and recommendations. Whether you're just starting your journey or looking for a medically guided approach, our team is here to help.

Which GLP-1 Medication Is Right for You?

Both medications are commonly used within medical weight management programs, but treatment recommendations vary based on individual health factors and goals. During your consultation, we'll determine which option may be most appropriate for you.

GLP-1 Receptor Agonist

Progress Looks Different for Everyone

What Results Can I Expect?

Weight management results vary from person to person and depend on factors such as starting weight, treatment adherence, lifestyle habits, physical activity, and individual health factors. Our goal is to support steady, sustainable progress rather than quick, short-term changes.

03

1–3 Months

Gradual progress may become more noticeable as healthy habits and treatment work together. Your provider will check in on your progress and make any necessary dosage adjustments.

04

3–6 Months

Continued adherence to treatment and lifestyle recommendations may lead to further improvements. Long-term monitoring and support continues throughout your program.

05

Ongoing Support & Long-Term Planning

Maintaining healthy lifestyle habits remains important both during and after treatment. Your provider can discuss long-term plans and maintenance strategies based on your individual goals.

Common Side Effects: Like any prescription medication, GLP-1 treatments may cause side effects in some individuals. Common side effects may include nausea, bloating, constipation, diarrhea, stomach discomfort, or reduced appetite. These effects are often temporary and may improve as your body adjusts to treatment. Your provider will review all potential risks, benefits, and safety considerations before starting therapy. Do not start any medication without proper medical evaluation.
